Eni S.p.A. is trading near 22.92 EUR as of early June 2026, supported by rising crude oil prices following Middle East tensions and the company's approval of the Baleine Phase 3 project in Cote d'Ivoire. The firm raised its 2026 cash flow guidance to 13.8 billion EUR and expanded its share buyback program to 2.8 billion EUR, while reporting 9% year-on-year upstream production growth in Q1. Third-party analyst targets cluster around 25.18-25.40 EUR per share, though the stock remains sensitive to commodity price fluctuations and downstream margin pressures.
